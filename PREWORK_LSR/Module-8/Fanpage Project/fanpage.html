Bio

Outkast (stylized as OutKast) is a hip-hop group formed out of high school in 1991 in East Point, Georgia. The group members are made up of Atlanta-based rappers Andre "Andre 3000" Benjamin (formerly known as Dre) and Antwan "Big Boi" Patton. The group achieved both critical acclaim and commercial success in the 1990s and early 2000s era. With their music, they help trend the style of Southern hip hop while also using diverse genres such as funk, psychedelia, jazz, and techno.

In 2000, Outkast released the critically acclaimed album Stankonia which included the hits "Ms. Jackson" and "B.O.B."" "In 2003, the duo released the double album Speakerboxxx/The Love Below, which featured the number one singles "Hey Ya!" and "The Way You Move." The album would eventually win the Grammy Award for Album of the Year and was certified Diamond by the Recording Industry Association of America," according to Wikipedia.

In 2006, they released the soundtrack for the musical movie Idlewild in which they starred in. In 2007, they took some time apart and pursued solo careers. In 2014, they reunited for their 20th anniversary at the worldwide Coachella Festival and performed at 40 other festivals. OutKast is one of the most successful hip hop groups of all time with receiving six Grammy Awards and selling over 25 million records between six studio albums and a greatest hits release.
